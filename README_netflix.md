# 🎬 Netflix Content Analysis

**Author:** Samuel Oluwadamilare Daramola  
**Tools:** Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn  
**Dataset:** 1,679 Netflix titles (2008–2021)

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs an exploratory data analysis (EDA) on Netflix's content catalogue to uncover patterns in content type, genre trends, country of origin, and audience rating distribution.

The goal is to answer key business questions such as:
- Is Netflix more movie-heavy or TV show-heavy — and is that changing?
- Which genres dominate the platform?
- Which countries produce the most content?
- Who is Netflix's target audience based on content ratings?

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

| # | Finding |
|---|---------|
| 1 | Movies make up **63.5%** of Netflix's catalogue; TV Shows **36.5%** — but TV Show additions are growing faster |
| 2 | **Drama** is the #1 genre, accounting for over 20% of all titles |
| 3 | Drama content grew by **~67%** over the analysis period, outpacing most other genres |
| 4 | The **United States** produces ~36% of all Netflix content — a significant concentration |
| 5 | **TV-MA** is the dominant rating (~32% of content), confirming Netflix's primary adult audience focus |
| 6 | African countries including Nigeria remain significantly underrepresented in the catalogue |
